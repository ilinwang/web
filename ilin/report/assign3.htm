Objective:

  • because the 2nd method can only send 1 unit once a time, which may take too long to finsih the algorithm.
  • In this new method, I send 1 demand as the 1st method, and using the concept of "researving" new fiber as 2nd method.t

How

  • Send 1 demand once a time as the 1st method; Reseave the next fiber when capacity becomes 0 as he 2nd method.
  • The main procedure is the combination of the 1st and 2nd method. Basically, we send 1 demand from the largest one to the smallest one. However, if a demand is really too large, and can't be feasible, we will have to add new fiber for all the arcs as the 1st method. If it is feasible, then, we scan for all the arcs on the assigned paths, if its capacity becomes 0 after the assignment, we will researve for next fiber, and change the capacity and cost as the 2nd method.
  • However, this is not as easy as the 2nd method when we want to update the network after assigning 1 demand. In the 2nd method, we can always guarantee the feasibility because the max flow it can send will be at least 1 unit. And also, it will only occupy single path. In the 3rd method, the result will be complicated, there may be a path with 'tree' structure. And also, 2 paths may use some arcs together as long as the capacity is allowed. So, how to update the nework after the assignmet will be very complicated.
  • Updating Network:
    • Do not update the network after we finish assign that demand
    • If we find the demand can't be satisfied, we will add 1 fiber for all the arcs, that is, capacity++, till we satisfy that demand
    • After the assignment, scan for all the arcs along the assigned paths,
      if the capacity beomces 0, we turn its flag ON, capacity=1, cost+=fiber_cost
      [Note: here for 1 arc, I at most only add 1 fiber]
      if the capacity does not become 0, we do nothing for that arc
    • Now, we may have to scan all the arcs whose flags just turn off
      For those which correspond to the same arc in the pysical network, we will only choose 1 to change the cost to include the new fiber fee[do not change its cost, because we already included in last step]; and then renew all the others' cost to be their original cost [cost-=fiber_cost]. [all these arcs' capacity=1, flag=ON].
  • Since we can not guarantee to satisfy a large demand, we may need to add new fiber overallly as the 1st method. Also, for the same sake, we have to use Simplex Algorithm as the 1st method.

Advantages:

  • Because I include the cost of "researving" a new fiber into this algorithm, the algorithm will not try to add a new fiber only if adding a new fiber costs less than traversing through other paths.Therefore, the result should be better than the 1st method.
  • Because I send 1 demand instead of 1 unit of demand, so, it should be faster than the 2nd method. But the result should not be as optimal as which of the 2nd method.

Disadvantages:

  • The way of adding new fibers when we can't satisfy a large demand is also the disadvantage of the 1st method.
  • It has more complicated updating network part than the 2nd method. Also, its result is not as optimal as the 2nd method
