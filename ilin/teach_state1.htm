Statement of Teaching Interests

I-Lin Wang

ilin@isye.gatech.edu

        Having been an OR graduate student in several of the best institutes for many years, I have learned and observed from many excellent teachers how to prepare good lectures, encourage students, design suitable homework sets and exams, and adjust teaching plans based on the feedback and input from students. My experience as a TA for one and half years at Georgia Tech has been beneficial to me. I have helped undergraduate students in operations research, probability and statistics courses. I have also given several lectures for graduate courses such as airline operations research and computational optimization. To ensure that my lecturing remains of good quality and in to keep up with the best schools in other countries, I plan to keep checking and updating my class materials by referencing on-line course materials from schools such as MIT, Stanford, and Georgia Tech.

        The following are the courses that I am qualified to teach:

  • Mathematics: Calculus, Linear Algebra, Probability, Statistics, Graph Theory, Discrete Math, Management Math
  • Deterministic OR: Linear Programming, Nonlinear Programming, Network Optimization, Integer Programming, Combinatorial Optimization
  • Stochastic OR: Stochastic Process, Simulations
  • Computations: C Programming, Data Structures, Algorithms, Computational Optimization
  • Applications: Supply Chain Modeling, Logistics, Transportation and Supply Chain Systems, Airline Operations Research

        Since my specialty is the creation and use of deterministic optimization techniques and algorithms, I am especially qualified to teach graduate level courses in the categories of Deterministic OR, Computations, and Applications as listed above. To teach new courses that cover different categories as listed above will also be fine with me.
